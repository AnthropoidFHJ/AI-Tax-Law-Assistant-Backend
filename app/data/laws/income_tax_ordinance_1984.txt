INCOME TAX ORDINANCE 1984 - BANGLADESH
=======================================

SECTION 44: Computation of Tax Payable

(1) The tax payable by an assessee shall be computed in accordance with the provisions of this section.

(2) For salaried individuals:
    (a) Basic exemption limit: BDT 350,000 for assessment year 2023-24
    (b) Tax slabs:
        - First BDT 350,000: 0% (exempted)
        - Next BDT 100,000 (350,001 to 450,000): 5%
        - Next BDT 400,000 (450,001 to 850,000): 10%
        - Next BDT 500,000 (850,001 to 1,350,000): 15%
        - Next BDT 650,000 (1,350,001 to 2,000,000): 20%
        - Above BDT 2,000,000: 25%

(3) Surcharge applicable:
    - For income above BDT 40,00,000: 10% surcharge on tax
    - For income above BDT 60,00,000: 15% surcharge on tax

SECTION 16: Allowable Deductions for Salaried Employees

The following deductions are allowed:
(a) House rent allowance (actual or 50% of basic salary, whichever is lower)
(b) Medical allowance up to BDT 120,000 per year
(c) Conveyance allowance up to BDT 30,000 per year
(d) Provident fund contribution by employee

SECTION 80: Tax Rebate on Investment

(1) Investment rebate available on:
    (a) Approved savings certificates: up to BDT 5,00,000
    (b) Life insurance premium: up to BDT 100,000
    (c) Contribution to recognized provident fund
    (d) Investment in stocks listed on stock exchange

(2) Rebate rate: 15% of eligible investment amount
(3) Maximum rebate cannot exceed 15% of total tax liability

ZERO RETURN PROVISIONS:

An assessee may submit zero return if:
- Total income does not exceed basic exemption limit
- No tax liability arises
- Required to maintain books of accounts under law

Mandatory e-filing for:
- Companies and firms
- Individuals with income above BDT 4,00,000
- Non-resident Bangladeshis
